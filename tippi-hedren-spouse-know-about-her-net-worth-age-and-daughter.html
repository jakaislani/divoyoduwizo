Tippi Hedren Spouse: Know About Her Net Worth, Age and Daughter

Tippi Hedren is an American actress, animal welfare activist and former model. Tippi Hedren is widely known for her work in the suspense-thriller “The Birds”, the psychological drama “Marnie” (1964) and her appearances as a front cover model in Life and Glamour magazines.

She was born to Bernard Carl and Dorothea Henrietta Hedren in New Ulm, Minnesota and moved to Minneapolis when she was four years old.

She used to take part in department store fashion shows during her high schooling. In her 20th birthday, she moved to New York, joined Eileen Ford Agency and began her career with the musical comedy “Petty Girl”.

During her career, she was prone to severe headaches which hampered her projects. Later, she got a titanium plate put in her neck which helped improve her condition.

The headaches again returned after a gallon of water fell into her head while rehearsing a scene. She had to file a suit to receive compensation for her inability to work.

Due to her lawyer’s negligence, she lost the case which again led her to file a case against her lawyer’s misconduct. In the end, she ended up not receiving any recompense for the damage.

Tippi Hedren – Movies and T.V Shows

Tippi Hedren had a highly successful modelling career in the 1950’s and the 1960’s. Hedren appeared in the covers of “The Saturday Evening Post” and McCall’s.

Alfred Hitchcock got fascinated by Tippi Hedren while watching her in a commercial for a diet drink called “Sego”.

Later, she was starred in the television series ”Alfred Hitchcock Presents”. Her acting skills were impressive which later landed her into the lead role in “The Birds”. Tippi later mentioned that Hitchcock was obsessed by her and assaulted her sexually.
